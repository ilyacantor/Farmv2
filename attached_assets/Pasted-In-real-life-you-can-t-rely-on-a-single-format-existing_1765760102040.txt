In real life you **can’t rely on a single “format” existing across sources**. IdP won’t give you domains. Finance may give merchants. CMDB gives display names. Sometimes you get URLs, sometimes not.

So the right real-world play is:

### Why you can’t “just pick a format”

There is no universal raw identifier. If you force everything to `*com`, you’ll be wrong for:

* products with no public domain signal in the record
* mergers / brand vs legal entity
* vendors with multiple domains
* internal apps (no domain)
* “Google+” / “Atlassian” / “Workday” style names

### What you do instead (real-life contract)

You define **one internal canonical key** and treat it as **an ID**, not a format:

**`vendor_key` = AutonomOS canonical vendor ID**
Stable, source-agnostic, deterministic.

Then you carry **evidence fields** alongside it:

* `display_name`
* `domains[]` (if known)
* `aliases[]`
* `source_keys[]` (what each plane called it)
* `key_confidence` (high/med/low)

### How reconciliation works in real life

Farm expectations should compare on **canonical vendor_key** (your internal ID), not domains or names.
If Farm wants to *show* `yammer.com`, that’s presentation, not identity.

### Practical “today” guidance for your synthetic world

Right now you have two “canonicals” fighting:

* Farm’s `*com` key
* AOD’s shorter key (`yammer`)

Don’t pretend either is “real life.” Pick one as **the internal ID** and make the other an alias.

**Best choice:** keep AOD’s `vendor_key` as the internal ID (e.g., `yammer`) and add `domains` / `domain_key` as evidence (e.g., `yammer.com`, `yammercom`). Then update Farm expected keys to be internal IDs, not domains.
