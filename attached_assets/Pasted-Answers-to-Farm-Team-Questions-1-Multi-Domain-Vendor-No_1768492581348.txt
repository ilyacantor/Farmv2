Answers to Farm Team Questions

1. Multi-Domain Vendor Normalization
Q: Does AOD consolidate multi-domain vendor aliases to their parent domain?

A: AOD does limited consolidation via ALIAS_DOMAINS_TO_COLLAPSE (e.g., googlemail.com → gmail.com). However, per Stage 4 fix (Jan 2026), several infrastructure domains were removed from collapse to preserve standalone keys:

outlook.com, office.com, gstatic.com - now kept as separate assets
cloudfront.net, awsstatic.com - preserved as standalone
AOD does NOT collapse googleapis.com → google.com. Each produces its own canonical key.

Q: Canonical key format?

A: AOD keys by entity name (e.g., Google Workspace) with multiple domains in identifiers.domains. The entity name comes from CMDB/IdP correlation or discovery observation name.

Recommendation: Farm should disable parent consolidation for infrastructure domains to match AOD.

2. Zombie Detection Logic
Q: What is AOD's zombie_window_days?

A: 90 days (configurable in policy_master.json under activity_windows.zombie_detection_days).

Q: How does AOD determine last activity?

A: AOD uses max of:

Discovery observation timestamps
IdP last_login_at
NOT finance timestamps (per policy: finance alone insufficient)
Q: Are zombies only for governed assets?

A: Yes. AOD zombie definition:

ZOMBIE = governed (has IdP OR has CMDB) AND STALE activity AND ongoing finance
Ungoverned + stale = PARKED, not zombie.

Q: Minimum evidence thresholds?

A: Yes - must have governance (CMDB or IdP) AND stale activity AND ongoing finance. The low zombie count (4 vs 25) suggests either:

Different governance determination
Different activity timestamp sources
Different finance threshold
3. Synthetic Domain Passthrough
Q: Additional domain filtering beyond policy exclusions?

A: Yes:

generic_collision_roots (cdn.com, edge.com, etc.) - suppressed unless anchored
shared_infrastructure_domains - mode: exclude/observe_only/include
custom_exclusions - operator-defined list
Q: Minimum observation thresholds?

A: Not for admission, but reason codes include DISCOVERY_SOURCE_COUNT_LT_2 vs DISCOVERY_SOURCE_COUNT_GE_2 for classification context.

Q: TLD pattern stripping?

A: No - AOD does not strip .dev, .app, .tech TLDs.

4. Admission Gate Alignment
Q: AOD's minimum admission criteria?

A: From policy_master.json:

In CMDB (authoritative match) → admit
In IdP (authoritative match) → admit
Has discovery + financially anchored → admit
Has discovery (2+ sources) + cloud evidence → admit
Additional gates:

noise_floor_days - observations must be within window
require_sso_or_ci_for_admission - optional gate
lifecycle_gates - CMDB lifecycle states that pass/fail
72 rejected difference likely from stricter lifecycle gates or noise floor.

5. False Positive Shadows (29 assets)
Q: How does AOD determine governance?

A:

is_governed = has_idp OR has_cmdb OR vendor_governed
Only one of CMDB or IdP is required for governance.

Q: CMDB lifecycle states treated as ungoverned?

A: Yes - lifecycle_gates in policy controls which CMDB states grant governance. States like pending, draft, retired may fail gates.

6. Policy Snapshot Verification
Q: Does AOD include policy_snapshot in run output?

A: Yes - policy_snapshot is stored in the runs table and available via /api/runs/{run_id}.

Q: Which takes precedence for reconciliation?

A: AOD is the authoritative source. Farm should consume AOD's policy_snapshot for reproducible grading.
