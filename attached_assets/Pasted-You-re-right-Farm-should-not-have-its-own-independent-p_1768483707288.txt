You’re right: **Farm should not have its own independent `policy_master.json`** in the sense of “Farm decides policy.”

What Farm *should* have is the ability to **consume AOD’s policy** for one purpose only: **generate and grade consistently**.

### Clean mental model

* **AOD owns policy** (switchboard = source of truth).
* **Farm is a harness**:

  * generates scenarios under a given policy
  * computes expected outcomes under that same policy
  * grades AOD against those expectations
* Farm never “overrides.” It either **loads** AOD policy or it **fails** with “missing policy”.

So the statement you gave Farm agent is directionally correct:

* ✅ AOD sets policy
* ✅ Farm aligns for grading only
* ✅ Farm cannot override

Two small wording fixes to keep it technically precise:

1. **“Policies are not logic”** — they *aren’t* code, but they *do* control logic.
   Better:

   > “Policies are configuration that control deterministic logic; they are not negotiated or inferred by Farm.”

2. TikTok ban is exactly the right example:

   * TikTok is a business SaaS / marketing platform → **should not be banned by default**
   * If a customer wants it excluded, that’s an explicit policy choice (rare).

### What this implies operationally (important)

Farm should not store its own “policy master,” but it does need **a policy snapshot per run** so grading is reproducible.

**Correct implementation pattern**

* When AOD runs a pipeline, it writes into the run artifact:

  * `policy_snapshot` (the effective policy JSON used)
  * or `policy_hash` + a pointer to the exact policy blob stored in AOD DB
* Farm reads that policy snapshot/hash when grading that run.

This avoids the “Farm and AOD aren’t aligned” class of bugs permanently.

### Bottom line

* **No**, Farm shouldn’t have independent policy authority.
* **Yes**, Farm must consume the exact AOD policy used for that run, as a snapshot, to generate/grade deterministically.
* And yes: **TikTok should not be banned**; it’s a business application domain, not shared infra.
